INSTRUCTIONS

Love stories needn’t always have to be cheesy or grand. In 2010, Satish Rajwade strode in the minds of Marathi viewers with the romantic Mumbai-Pune-Mumbai. The film clicked due to its unconventionality. It broke away from clichéd love stories and mushy romance to present a practical yet lovable couple. In that sense, Rajwade has always experimented with the unconventional through his films. So it doesn’t come as a surprise that Ti Sadhya Kay Karte (TSKK) treads the same path. But the film is still as relatable, as simple and as sweet as the director’s previous ventures. TSKK is spread over three generations and has three sets of actors portraying the same characters- Anurag and Tanvi. The whole focus of the film is around first love and it unfolds through the eyes of the present day Anurag (Chaudhary). The viewers are taken into flashback and introduced to our lead characters who are then just getting to know each other. This transforms into friendship in college, a time of confusion for Anurag and realisation for Tanvi, and simultaneously the grown up duo that reminisces about their childhood. The love stories we’ve grown up watching have conditioned us to believe in the protagonists ending up together, but TSKK doesn’t do that and that’s a much-needed break from the usual. Because, we’ve had enough of ‘ek ladka aur ek ladki kabhi dost nahi ho sakte’ and it’s time we accept the fact that certain relations transcend definition.
